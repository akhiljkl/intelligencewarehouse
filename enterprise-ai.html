What the Data Actually Says

The question isn't whether to use AI. It's whether to build the context layer yourself or deploy one that's already proven.

Only 6% of organizations qualify as "AI high performers" with >5% EBIT impact. The rest are stuck in experiment mode. The single strongest predictor of success? Fundamentally redesigning workflows—not better models.

Purchasing AI tools from specialized vendors succeeds 67% of the time. Internal builds succeed only ~22%. A 3x success rate gap. The conclusion: foundational infrastructure should be bought, not built.
Gartner — Agentic AI Forecast ↗
40%+
More than 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 due to escalating costs, unclear business value, and inadequate risk controls. The projects that survive share one trait: structured context.
Industry Aggregate ↗
80%
Of a custom software's total cost occurs after initial launch. Maintenance alone consumes 50–75% of total cost. Each enhancement costs 3–4x the original development. The build path doesn't get cheaper—it compounds.
Foundation Capital ↗
"The next trillion-dollar platforms won't be built by adding AI to existing data. They'll be built by capturing the decision traces that make data actionable."
Enterprise value is shifting from systems of record to systems of agents. The missing layer is the Context Graph—decision traces stitched across entities and time, where precedent becomes searchable. Systems of record capture what happened, but agents need access to the why: how rules were applied, where exceptions were granted, how conflicts were resolved, who approved what. That's what Intelligence Warehouse captures.
Atlan — Gartner MQ Leader ↗
"No agent can reason effectively about a business it doesn't understand."
Atlan validates the category: AI agents need a context layer to understand what your data means, how teams work, how your company defines "revenue." Without it, agents produce semantically wrong outputs at scale. Atlan focuses on metadata and data definitions. Intelligence Warehouse goes further—capturing the decision logic, rules, thresholds, and authority chains that drive actual operations.
